# Music Cape Helper

A RuneLite plugin that helps you complete the music log and unlock the **music cape**. Shows all music tracks organized by area, with unlock hints for tracks you haven't found yet — and guides you to each track's unlock spot on the world map and in the game world.

## Features

- Side panel listing all music tracks with their area
- Track name, unlock status (green = unlocked, red = locked), and unlock hint
- "Missing only" toggle to show just what you need
- Search/filter by track name
- Summary counts (total unlocked / total tracks)
- Unlock state persisted between sessions
- **Wiki mode** – click any track to open its OSRS Wiki page for full details
- **Map mode** – click any track to mark its unlock location:
  - The **world map** jumps to the spot and shows the unlock **area highlighted**, for every known location of the track
  - If the map is closed, a flashing **infobox** reminds you to open it
  - When you get near the spot, the tile is **highlighted on the ground** with the track name, and a **hint arrow** points to it — this works in **dungeons and caves** too, where the world map can't reach
  - **Underground tracks** (e.g. Sarachnis in Forthos Dungeon) mark the **surface area directly above the dungeon** on the world map with a red **UNDERGROUND** badge, and the dungeon outline projected onto the ground above — head there, find the entrance, and the in-game highlight takes over once you're inside
  - **Map areas**: when a track's exact spot is shown by one of the world map's own areas (the **map list** at the bottom of the map: Gielinor Surface, Kourend Underground, Mor Ul Rek, …), the chat message names the entry to pick, and the plugin **highlights the map list button, the right entry** once the list is open — with **scroll up/down arrows** when the entry is out of view — and centers the map on the spot when you select the area. This works from anywhere: standing in the Wizards' Tower basement you can still be guided to a surface track, and vice versa
  - **Self-contained zones** (Rat Pits, God Wars Dungeon, Zanaris, minigames, …) mark the zone's **surface entrance** instead, with a note on how to get in
  - Instanced areas the map can't represent at all open the wiki, with a chat message naming the unlock place (from the wiki) and how it's accessed
  - Every guidance message ends with the track's own in-game **unlock hint**
- **✕ button** to clear the marker, highlights and hint arrow

## Why?

The in-game music player already shows locked tracks, but it's tedious to scroll through every area and read each tooltip one by one. This plugin lists all unlock hints at once, and then actually takes you there: pick a missing track, jump the world map to its unlock area, and follow the on-the-ground highlight for the final steps — like the clue scroll helper, but for music tracks.

## Usage

1. Install and enable the plugin
2. Open the **Music tab** (keyboard shortcut or click the music icon)
3. The **Music Cape Helper** panel populates from the Music tab data
4. Click the music note icon in the RuneLite sidebar to open the panel
5. Toggle "Missing only" to filter to locked tracks
6. Use the search box to find specific tracks
7. Switch between **Wiki** and **Map** at the top of the panel, then click a track

> **Note:** The plugin observes the vanilla Music tab to determine which tracks are unlocked. Open the Music tab with the "All" filter selected at least once so every track gets evaluated. The unlocked state is cached between sessions.

## Configuration

Day-to-day controls (missing-only filter, Wiki/Map click mode, search, clear marker) live directly in the side panel. The settings page only holds the in-game guidance options:

- **Highlight location in scene** – Ground highlight when near the unlock spot
- **Show hint arrow** – Minimap arrow when near the unlock spot
- **Highlight area on world map** – Draw the unlock area as a region on the world map
- **Highlight color** – Color used for surface highlights and markers
- **Underground color** – Color used for the surface-above-a-dungeon markers

## Contributing: improving the track data

The plugin ships four data files in `src/main/resources`, all generated from the [OSRS Wiki](https://oldschool.runescape.wiki):

| File | Contents | Generated by |
|---|---|---|
| `track_coords.json` | Unlock locations per track: `{"Track": [{"name", "center": [x,y,plane], "polygon": [[x,y],…], "mapId"}]}` | `scripts/scrape_track_coords.py` |
| `wiki_locations.json` | Human-readable unlock place per track (wiki infobox `location` field) | `scripts/scrape_wiki_locations.py` |
| `map_areas.json` | The world map's own areas (map list entries): name, bounds, and the 64×64 squares each area really renders | `scripts/gen_map_areas.py` (curated tables inside) |
| `map_entrances.json` | Surface entrance per named zone: `{"<mapId>": {"name", "entrance": [x,y]\|null, "note"}}` | `scripts/gen_map_entrances.py` (curated tables inside) |

What each script does:

1. **`scrape_track_coords.py`** — walks every page in the wiki's *Category:Music_tracks* and extracts unlock locations from `{{Map|…}}` kartographer templates (inline and on `Map:<Track>` subpages): polygon outlines, centers, planes, and the wiki `mapID` of the map each highlight sits on. Historical features (removed content) are skipped. Tracks the wiki marks as *Missing track location* get an intentionally empty list.
2. **`scrape_wiki_locations.py`** — grabs each track's infobox `location` field ("Tombs of Amascut") so the plugin can say where a track unlocks when the map can't show it.
3. **`gen_map_areas.py`** — builds the in-game map area index: names from the wiki's mapID table (they match the in-game map list labels), bounds from the wiki map engine config, and each area's actually-rendered squares probed from the wiki tile server (a missing tile = an empty square). Areas newer than the engine snapshot use the curated `CURATED_BOUNDS` boxes.
4. **`gen_map_entrances.py`** — assigns each mid-band zone location (Rat Pits, Mor Ul Rek, minigames — the y 4160–6400 band) its named map: nearest wiki map center within 160 tiles first, bounds containment second, validated so a wrong id can never survive. Emits the entrance file from the **hand-curated `ENTRANCES` table**. Needs the other three outputs, so run it last.

### Automated updates

```bash
pip install requests
python3 scripts/scrape_track_coords.py      # 1. full re-scrape of all track pages (~10 min, rate-limited)
python3 scripts/scrape_wiki_locations.py    # 2. refresh unlock place names
python3 scripts/gen_map_areas.py            # 3. rebuild the map area index (probes ~900 tiles, ~1 min)
python3 scripts/gen_map_entrances.py        # 4. re-assign zones + regenerate entrances (needs 1-3)
```

The scrapers are polite (0.35 s between requests) — please keep them that way. Rebuild with `./gradlew jar` and side-load the jar to verify before opening a PR.

### Manual fixes

All human-maintained data lives in small curated tables at the top of the two `gen_*` scripts — fix the table, re-run the script, rebuild:

- **A track is missing coordinates**: check its wiki page for a `{{Map|…}}` template or a `Map:<Track>` subpage. If the wiki has the data but the scraper misses it, that's a scraper bug — please open an issue with the track name. If the wiki has no map, the fix belongs on the wiki itself: add the location there (that helps everyone, not just this plugin) and re-run the scraper.
- **A track points at the wrong spot**: same rule — fix the wiki page, re-run the scraper.
- **A zone entrance is wrong or missing** (red UNDERGROUND marker in a bad place, or a "See the wiki for access" note): edit `ENTRANCES` in `gen_map_entrances.py` — one line per zone: `mapId: ([x, y], "how to get in")` or `(None, "access note")` for teleport-only zones.
- **A track is matched to the wrong zone**: add it to `MAPID_OVERRIDES` in `gen_map_entrances.py` (`"Track": mapId` or `None` for no zone).
- **An instance has no map anywhere** (marker would land on meaningless ground): add its unlock place to `PLACE_ENTRANCES` in `gen_map_entrances.py` with a surface entrance, e.g. the Temple of the Eye → the Wizards' Tower portal.
- **The wrong map list entry is suggested / a marker lands on black**: adjust the area's box in `CURATED_BOUNDS` in `gen_map_areas.py` (only areas newer than the engine snapshot use these). If an in-game area was renamed or removed, `SKIP_STALE_IDS`/`VALIDATION_EXEMPT` in the same file control how the snapshot entry is treated.
- Coordinates are world tiles; you can read them off the wiki map URLs or in game with RuneLite's Developer Tools. Surface y is below 4160; dungeons in the classic band are exactly 6400 above the ground they sit under; the 4160–6400 band holds self-contained zones and instances.
